Newton’s Laws

This topic is conceptual and intuitive rather than purely mathematical.

Laws of Motion

  1. An object at rest remains at rest, and an object in motion remains in motion at constant speed in a straight line unless acted on by an unbalanced force.
  2. The acceleration of an object depends on the mass of the object and the amount of force applied.
  3. Whenever one object exerts a force on another, the second object exerts an equal and opposite force on the first.

Second Law

A force can be some influence that can change the state of motion of a body.Momentum(p) is something which can tell about the motion of a body(p=mv)and force can change the state of motion of a body hence distupting momentum and bringing change in momentum-time graph or just change in momentum with passage of time. So, Mathematically:

\[ F = \frac{dp}{dt} \]

If mass remains constant, we get:

\[ F = ma \]
This formula only works with cases where mass is constant but 99% of the time it is constant.
Note: Try deriving \(F = ma \) using \( F = \frac{dp}{dt} \) on your own! Rememeber, mass remains constant

Springs

You will learn more about springs later but now you must have the basic idea. A spring is a device which can be stretched or compressed from its stretched position(mean position) and applies a force opposite to the direction in which it has been displaced i.e it pushes outwards if it is compressed but pulls inwards if stretched.The more it is stretched, the more force it applies. therefore, we can say that:

\[ F_s \propto x \] \[ \begin{align} F_s &= -k x \end{align} \]

Where Fs is the force applied by a spring, x is the displacement from mean position and k is proportionality constant which varies spring to spring.

Note: The -ve sign is to demonstrate the opposite direction of the force.

Inertia and Frames Of Refrences

Inertia is basically the unwilligness of an object to change it's state of motion. This is basically Newton’s 1st law. Inertia basically means that an object will be in rest or in motion if left undisturbed but this does not mean it will resist change on experiencing an external force. One cool example of inertia is When you are standing in a bus and suddenly brakes are applied, you tend to fall forward. Why? because your lower half of the body comes to rest while the upper half continues to be in motion due to inertia as no external force is applied on your upper half of the body

Frames Of Refrences

A Frame of refrence is an abstract system from where we work our forces and basically do our calculation. One important case of this is an inertial frame of refrence.

An inertial frame of refrence is a refrence where the net forces acting on a body is zero i.e it is in equilibrium. This means it's either in rest or in uniformly acclelerated motion.

Choosing a frame

A frame should be chosen according to the needs of the problem but usually inertial works out best.

Pseudo Forces

It is a fake force used in non-inertial frames to work out correct calculations.

Its formula is:

\[ F_f = m a_f \]

Where m is the mass of the object, and af is the acceleration of the non-inertial frame.
